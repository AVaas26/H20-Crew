The Water Project

The main intention of The water Project to people is to make sure that people get the pure and healthy drinking water specially in the areas with low supply or crisis. Water is crucial for human life and, yet, some communities all over the world don’t have sufficient access to this basic thing because of various reasons like poverty, environment damage, or loss of infrastructure. To meet this urgent demand, donors aim to mitigate the short-run suffering, protect from diseases that are easily transmitted through water and to help communities which need support, whether because of natural disasters, conflicts or due to chronic economic issues. As well as that, those initiatives commonly strive to develop durable options for water use and facilities, with a purpose of establishing permanent positive effects on the nice of life, health as well as well-being of these communities’ members.

Clean water changes everything

Education

Clean water boosts education. It prevents them from being ill, therefore seldom missing classes. They keep being hydrated to concentrate more in their lesson. Time that would have been used obtaining water is now dedicated to studying. When there is clean water for hand washing, disease transmission in schools is reduced. All in all, clean water helps to create a better and safe community that the students can prosper in.

Health

Availability of safe water has confidence saving lives. Infected water is a tool for, disease transmission such as diarrhea, cholera, and dysentery. Besides many other illness which one can suffer in the case there is no clean drinking water available, the sole risk of drinking clean water is that eventually it would put an end to the risk of diseases and would lead to a high level of immunity. Tanzania stands to enjoy potable water that is a factor of healthy body functioning. This is important as it enhances wellness and health in individuals.

Time

Absolutely! Apart from the sole utility of temporary water supply, it lessen the time spent in gathering water from the areas that lack availability to clean supply of water. Even walking, once in a while, instead of driving helps in the economization of the time needed to withdraw and clean the water closer to home. Waterborne diseases are the top reason for recurring sickness episodes, causing resigned people to skip work or school. Swallowing clean water on the part of most of the population doesn't just mean less diseases but also more hours for production.

Poverty

The access to safe water remained the weapon for poverty reduction. It provides diligent employees with a good health. So, they can stay with the company. Time saved as people march for clean water access is a time spent working in jobs or school. Providing pure irrigation and having safe and healthy crops will contribute either for individuals to eat or to sell and earn cash from crop production. After all, if the water is clean then there is less sickness and more being done. This makes the lives of the people better and allows them to get above the vicious circle of poverty.

What they Did

The Water Project is focused in four geographic regions across Kenya, Uganda, and Sierra Leone

Western Kenya

Through The Water Project’s Wash program, abundant water percolating from the two seasonal rains that flow through a myriad of springs with high water tables and deep aquifers in the Western Kenya area will be accessed, protected, filtered, and disinfected to meet the needs of residents. Evaluate water programs in villages, schools, and places of worship in Western Kenya such protected springs, rain water catchment systems, and water wells. The concept of the program lies in the principle of equal geographic propagation of the activities, qualitative training in hygiene and sanitation, and the contacts among health workers, local officials and educators.

Southeastern Kenya

The Water Project’s WaSH initiative in Southeast Kenya is about ending the challenge of water scarcity by building sand dams, shallow hand dug wells, and 104,000 liter rainwater catchments system in a semi-arid environment. Investigate activities undertaken in this region to ascertain how you can avail yourself of (being an active team member in) such approaches as farming innovation, education, hygiene and sanitation training, and reliable water supply.

Uganda

The Wash program by Water Project in West of Uganda is designed to be a progress to the people in giving them the sense of ownership of the clean water access points put in place and the hygiene & sanitation behavior change. Consider water projects like secure the springs with protective methods and water wells to provide village wide water access totally coverage and a household level analysis of sanitation and hygiene practices. This community level intervention takes by a one of kind method that links the Water Carer Committees (these tasked with the responsibility of caring for the water points) with a Village Savings and Loans Association.

Sierra Leone

The Water Project’s Wash program in Port Loko district, Sierra Leone is made up of a wide web that directly serves both new and rehabilitated water wells. Immerse yourself into these projects that are resilient as they have top level implementations, tailor made hygiene and sanitation training, and credible monitoring, evaluation, and solutions relationships.
